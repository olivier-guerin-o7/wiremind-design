Methodology · transparency How every number is computed

Attribution. For each route × day, we compute the difference between actual revenue and the pre-CAYZN baseline model. Lift = ∑(actual − baseline). YTD figures are point-in-time actuals.

Trajectory. Linear projection × last-year same-period seasonality factor. Jun–Aug carries a +15% multiplier based on Transavia's 2024–25 observed pattern. Ranges, not points. Every forward figure is labelled "trajectory", never a guarantee.

Resource forecasts. Daily rolling average × remaining days, seasonality-adjusted. Cap date = the day the trajectory line crosses the contracted limit.

Sensitivity. Open the Outcomes card to re-compute attribution with a different baseline assumption and scope.

Insight · Risk Dual cap · Aug 12

GenAI Tokens and Ancillary Tokens are both projected to reach their annual cap during the same week of August 12, 2026. At current burn rates, GenAI hits its 1.5B limit on Aug 12 and Ancillary hits its 2.0M limit on Aug 14 — a two-day overlap.

Simultaneous exhaustion means the live pricing engine loses both its AI scoring and ancillary recommendation layers at once, significantly degrading quote quality during one of the peak summer booking weeks.

A top-up on GenAI alone (~€3.2K) would push its cap past end-of-year and break the overlap. Alternatively, a combined top-up covers both for ~€4.8K — less than the estimated revenue impact of a 5-day degraded pricing window.

Insight · Cost €9K overage incoming

Current GenAI trajectory projects end-of-year usage at ~1.85B tokens — 23% above the contracted 1.5B limit. At plan overage rates, this translates to approximately €9,200 in additional charges billed at contract renewal.

A pre-cap top-up of 400M tokens is priced at ~€3,200 — roughly 65% cheaper than paying the overage rate. Acting before August 5 locks in current pricing; after that date, emergency top-ups apply a 15% surcharge.

Net saving by acting now: ~€5,800. The top-up would also prevent the dual-cap risk flagged in the Risk insight.

Insight · Context Above seasonal norm

F&O Executions burn rate is currently 27% above the median for comparable airlines on the Premium tier. This compares your month-8 cumulative usage against 11 anonymised peer accounts with similar fleet sizes and route networks.

Last year's summer effect (Jun–Aug 2025) added +18% to F&O usage over the same period. If this seasonal pattern repeats, current trajectory projects F&O hitting its 15M annual cap by early September — three months ahead of schedule.

This is a context signal, not an immediate action item. No overage risk exists today, but monitoring is recommended. Consider setting a usage alert at 80% (currently at 63%) to get 30 days' notice.
